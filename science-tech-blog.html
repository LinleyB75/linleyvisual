English for Science & Tech Professionals

Engineers, developers, and scientists often have a unique problem: Your technical vocabulary is excellent, but your "connective" language is tricky.

You know the specific terms for your field (algorithms, throughput, thermodynamics), but explaining why those things matter to a non-technical manager can be frustrating.

The Gap Between Tech and Business

The goal of English in a technical role is usually simplification. You need to take complex ideas and make them accessible.

  • Bad: "The latency issues are arising due to the asynchronous callback functions not executing in the correct stack order."
  • Good: "The system is slow because one part of the code is waiting too long for another part to finish. We need to sync them up."

If you can master the art of "translating" your technical knowledge into simple business English, you become invaluable to your company.

In our lessons, we practice this translation. I play the "manager" who doesn't understand the tech, and you practice explaining it simply.
