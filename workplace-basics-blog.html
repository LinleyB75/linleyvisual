Workplace English: Technical to Talk

You can write a perfect report, but can you chat before the meeting starts?

This is the "Workplace Paradox." Many professionals are excellent at their specific job in English (Accounting, Logistics, IT) because the vocabulary is repetitive. You know the words for your industry.

But when the meeting ends and someone asks, "So, do you have any plans for the weekend?"... you freeze.

Why "Small Talk" is Actually "Big Talk"

In many cultures, small talk is seen as a waste of time. In English-speaking business culture (especially Australia, UK, and USA), small talk is trust-building.

If you only speak about work, you can seem cold or robotic. If you can joke or chat about the weather for 2 minutes, colleagues feel more comfortable with you. When they feel comfortable, they trust your professional opinions more.

The "Ford" Technique

Not sure what to talk about? Remember FORD:

  • F - Family/Friends: "Did you visit your parents this weekend?"
  • O - Occupation: "How is that new project going?"
  • R - Recreation: "Did you watch the match on Sunday?"
  • D - Dreams/Days: "Any holidays planned for summer?"

Moving from Textbook to Reality

Textbooks teach you to say: "Hello, how are you? I am fine, thank you."

Real workplaces say: "Hey, how's it going? Not too bad, you?"
