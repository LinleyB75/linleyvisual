Workplace English: Technical to Talk

Moving from a technical role to a management role is one of the hardest transitions for non-native speakers. Why? Because the language changes.

In a technical role, you speak with facts, data, and code. In a management role, you must speak with diplomacy, persuasion, and clarity.

Softening Your Language

Technical language is often direct: "This won't work." or "That is wrong."

Management language is often softer to maintain relationships: "I see a potential risk with that approach," or "Have we considered the alternative?"

Many of my students feel "fake" using this language at first. But it is not about being fake; it is about being effective. If you are too direct, people may become defensive and stop listening to your good ideas.
