Patience & Listening for Beginners

If you have just started learning Business English, you might feel frustrated. You want to speak confidently in meetings now, but the words just won't come out.

This frustration is normal. But if you let it control you, you will quit. The key to success is not speedâ€”it is Patience and Listening.

"You cannot output what you have not input. Speaking starts with listening."

Why You Can't Speak Yet

Imagine a cup. If you pour water into it (listening), eventually it will overflow (speaking). But many students try to force the water out before the cup is full.

If you can't find the words, it means you haven't heard them enough times yet. You need to absorb more English before you can produce it.

The Power of "Active Listening"

Don't just listen to English as background noise. Do Active Listening:

  • Listen for Phrases: Don't listen to individual words. Listen for chunks like "As a result," "In my opinion," or "Let's move on."
  • Listen for Intonation: Does the speaker's voice go up or down at the end of a sentence? Copy that music.
  • Listen for Emotion: Is the speaker confident? Hesitant? Polite? How do their words change based on their feeling?

Be Patient with Yourself

Learning a language for the workplace is a marathon, not a sprint. You will have bad days where you feel like you can't speak at all. That is okay.

Focus on 1% improvement every day. Did you learn one new phrase today? Did you understand one email a little faster? That is a win.

If you stay patient and keep listening, the speaking will come naturally.
